About our Dress Code

Our dress code is designed to keep dancers safe and distraction-free by having proper, well-fitting footwear and attire. Our studio aims to make dance accessible for everyone, so our dress code is gender-agnostic. Dancers may choose from any option listed for their class below. Please email office@eastside-dance.com or text/call 425.636.8494 with any questions.

Where to Shop

We recommend Dancewear Center, located 1.5 miles from Eastside Dance, on 85th Street in Kirkland. If this is your first time wearing a specific dance shoe, we recommend getting fitted at Dancewear Center. Fittings are free, walk-ins are welcome, and their staff will ensure your dancer is wearing a safe, well-fitting, high-quality shoe.

What to Wear

  • Attire: Please wear the clothing specified for your class below. Dancers may choose any option listed.
  • Shoes:  Please purchase the specified dance shoe(s) for their class(es). Write the dancer's name on all shoes - they get mixed up easily! Dance shoes should only be worn in the lobby/studio to protect our specialized Marly floors.
  • Hair: Hair should be pulled back out of the dancer's face (e.g. ponytail, braids, twists for long hair, or clips/headband for short hair). Ballet dancers must have their hair secured into a bun. (Here is a tutorial video for different hair types.)
  • Jewelry:  Please no jewelry or accessories in class for safety.
  • Water bottle: Bring your water bottle into the studio with you.

Attire: Dancewear (e.g. leotard, tights) or clothes the dancer can easily move in (e.g. leggings, shorts, t-shirts). 


Shoes: Dancers can wear bare feet or ballet shoes. No socks as dancers may slip. If the ballet shoes have drawstrings, drawstrings should be double-knotted and cut off at the knot for safety. Caregivers can wear bare feet or socks. For orthopedic issues, clean sneakers or dance shoes are welcome.

Attire:


  • Leotard and tights (pink or skin tone) worn under the leotard OR leotard, t-shirt, or athletic top worn with well-fitting athletic bottoms (e.g. bike shorts, athletic shorts, leggings, athletic joggers, or jazz pants).
  • Dance skirts and tutus are ok if they are attached to the leotard. Please no tie skirts or pull-on skirts/tutus as they often come untied or distract dancers during class.

Hair and Undergarments:


  • Hair should be pulled back out of the dancer's face (e.g. ponytail, braids, twists for long hair, or clips/headband for short hair)
  • No undergarments are worn under a leotard, just like wearing a bathing suit. A special gusset is sewn into the leotard to provide sanitary protection.

Shoes: Dancers need black tap shoes AND either ballet shoes or jazz shoes. Ballet/jazz shoes should match your tights color (i.e. pink/skin color) or be black if wearing dance bottoms like shorts or leggings. Shoe drawstrings should be double-knotted, cut one inch from the knot, and tucked into the shoe for safety. Tap shoes should be slip-on or have elastic or velcro closures. All tap ties need to be removed for safety. Please write the dancer's name on both sets of shoes.
